Encebollado

Description

Encebollado is a dish that is commonly cooked to relieve hangovers and is also called chuchaqui in Ecuador. Encebollado is a broth based on fish (white tuna), which also contains cassava, tomato, onion, pepper and other ingredients. This dish is usually served with popcorn, chifles (thin strips of fried green plantain), tostado (toasted corn), or bread. You cannot miss this traditional dish during your stay in the country.


Ingredients

  • 1 Tuna steak
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 1 green pepper
  • 5 red onions
  • 2 celery stalks
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 small bunch of coriander
  • 1 tablespoon of dry chili
  • 2 cassavas
  • 6 lemons
  • oil
  • cumin
  • salt
  • black pepper

Preparation

  1. Place the fish in a pot with cold water, bring it to a boil and skim.
  2. Then add the chopped tomato, 2 red onions, celery, green pepper, garlic, tied cilantro stems, aj√≠ de seco, salt, pepper and cumin.
  3. Cook over low heat for approximately 25 minutes.
  4. Remove the fish, wait for it to cool down, press it lightly to laminate it and reserve it.
  5. Blend the tuna cooking liquid together with the vegetables that were cooked.
  6. Chop 3 red onions into julienne strips, rinse them in water, and add lemon juice and salt to tan them.
  7. After tanning add oil, black pepper and chopped coriander.
  8. Cook the cassava in large pieces in salted water, once cooked, cut it into cubes.
  9. Finish the dish by mixing the cut cassava, add the sliced tuna, pickled onion and little by little the blended cooking liquid until it is consistent.
